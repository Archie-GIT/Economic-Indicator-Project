# Economic-Indicator-Project
Developed an end-to-end economic indicators analysis using SQL for data exploration and Microsoft Power BI for interactive visualizations. 
Built dashboards highlighting GDP growth, regional comparisons, and correlations between economic development, internet access, and life expectancy.

---

# Short Description / Purpose

The Development Indicators Dashboard is an interactive data analytics project designed to explore global economic and social development trends across countries and regions. The dashboard analyzes key indicators such as GDP, life expectancy, unemployment, birth rate, and internet usage to help users understand relationships between economic growth, health outcomes, and technological development worldwide.

This tool is intended for use by data analysts, economists, policymakers, and researchers who want to quickly identify global development patterns, regional disparities, and socio-economic relationships using interactive visual analytics.

---

# Tech Stack

The dashboard was built using the following tools and technologies:

• 📊 **Microsoft Power BI Desktop** – Main platform used for designing interactive dashboards and visualizations.
• 🗄 **SQL** – Used for data exploration, analytical queries, aggregation, and trend analysis of development indicators.
• 📂 **Power Query** – Used for data cleaning, transformation, and preparing the dataset before analysis.
• 🧠 **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)** – Used to create calculated measures such as average GDP, unemployment rates, and KPI indicators.
• 🧩 **Data Modeling** – Structured the dataset to enable efficient aggregation and filtering by country, region, and income groups.
• 📁 **File Format** – `.pbix` for Power BI development and `.png` for dashboard previews.

---

# Data Source

**Source:** World Bank Development Indicators dataset.
The dataset includes global development metrics for multiple countries across several decades. It contains economic, demographic, and technological indicators such as GDP, unemployment rates, life expectancy, birth and death rates, infant mortality rates, and internet usage.

These indicators allow comparative analysis across countries, regions, and economic classifications.

---

#. Features / Highlights

## • Business Problem
Global development data contains large volumes of economic and social indicators, making it difficult to quickly understand patterns and relationships between income, health, employment, and technology.

Questions such as:

* Which regions have the highest economic output?
* How does GDP relate to life expectancy?
* Which countries experience the highest unemployment rates?
* How does internet access relate to economic growth?
* How do demographic indicators vary across income groups?
They 
are difficult to analyze using raw data tables alone.

---

## • Goal of the Dashboard

The goal of the dashboard is to provide an **interactive analytical platform** that:

* Enables exploration of global economic and social development indicators.
* Helps users compare countries and regions across key metrics.
* Reveals relationships between income, health, and technology.
* Supports data-driven insights for policy analysis, research, and economic studies.

# Walkthrough of Key Visuals

### Global KPI Indicators

Displays key summary statistics:

* Average GDP
* Average Life Expectancy
* Average Unemployment Rate
* Average Birth Rate
* Average Death Rate
* Average Infant Mortality Rate

These KPIs provide a quick overview of global development conditions.

---

### GDP by Region (Bar Chart)

Compares total GDP across global regions to highlight economic concentration and regional economic strength.

---

### GDP Growth Over Time (Line Chart)

Shows long-term economic growth trends across decades, enabling users to analyze how global economic output has evolved.

---

### Top Countries by GDP (Bar Chart)

Ranks the leading economies based on GDP levels, allowing users to quickly identify the largest contributors to global economic activity.

---

### GDP vs Life Expectancy (Scatter Plot)

Analyzes the relationship between national income and health outcomes.
Higher GDP levels generally correlate with higher life expectancy, demonstrating the link between economic development and public health.

---

### GDP vs Internet Users (Scatter Plot)

Examines how digital connectivity relates to economic performance, revealing that countries with higher internet penetration often exhibit stronger economic output.

---

### Countries with Highest Unemployment (Bar Chart)

Highlights countries experiencing the highest unemployment rates, allowing analysts to identify labor market challenges.

---

### Regional Social Development Indicators

Visual comparisons of:

* Life expectancy by region
* Unemployment by region

These charts highlight regional disparities in social and economic well-being.

---

### Income Group Filters

Interactive slicers allow users to filter data by income classifications such as:

* Low income
* Lower middle income
* Upper middle income
* High income

This enables deeper analysis of development differences across economic groups.

# • Business Impact & Insights

**Economic Analysis:** Analysts can identify which regions drive global economic output and how economic power is distributed worldwide.

**Public Health Insights:** The relationship between GDP and life expectancy highlights how economic development contributes to improved health outcomes.

**Digital Development:** Internet usage analysis reveals the connection between technological adoption and economic growth.

**Labor Market Monitoring:** The unemployment ranking helps identify countries with potential economic or structural employment challenges.

**Policy and Research Applications:** Policymakers and researchers can use the dashboard to analyze development patterns, identify regional disparities, and evaluate socio-economic progress.
